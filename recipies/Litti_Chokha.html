Litti Chokha

It is a bihari dish and my personal faviorite, it is almost liked by entire bihar and all bihari around the world no matter where are they. And if someone claim that he is a bihar and doesn't like litti chokha then he is not a bihari.

Above paragraph is just the most common thing you will heard from a bihari about the litti chokha, this food is most famous and easy to find in bihar. Everyone have their own way to make and eat this food but today i am going tell you the best way to prepare this dish. soo lets go.......



Ingredients

  • for dough:
  • 1½ cup wheat flour / atta
  • ¼ tsp baking powder
  • ¼ tsp ajwain / carom seeds
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp ghee / clarified butter
  • water for kneading
  • for stuffing:
  • 1 cup sattu / roasted gram flour
  • 2 tbsp coriander, finely chopped
  • 1 chilli, finely chopped
  • ½ tsp ginger garlic paste
  • ½ tsp cumin / jeera
  • ½ tsp kalonji / onion seeds / nigella seeds
  • ¼ tsp ajwain / carom seeds
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • 2 tbsp mustard oil
  • for tomato chokha:
  • 3 tomato
  • 1 chilli, finely chopped
  • 2 clove garlic, finely chopped
  • 1 inch ginger, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp coriander, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • 2 tbsp mustard oil
  • ¼ tsp salt

Steps

  1. pinch a small ball sized dough. press the edges and form a cup.
  2. further place a ball sized sattu stuffing in the centre.
  3. start pleating the dough, making sure the stuffing is secured well.
  4. pinch off excess dough and roll well.
  5. place the rolled litti in appe pan heated with oil. or preheat and bake in oven at
  6. 180 degree celcius for 45 minutes flipping in between.
  7. cover and cook on low flame for 10 minutes.
  8. flip over and cook both sides. cook for approx 30 minutes in total
  9. continue to cook until the litti turns golden brown from all the sides and cooked from inside
  10. finally, litti is ready to serve with chokha and ghee.
